Third Bank Amendment
Section 1. There shall be a Bank of the United States, responsible for the monetary policy of the United States, the maintenance of financial stability, the chartering of national banks and thrifts, the regulation of national banks, securities, insurance, thrifts, and other commercial instruments delegated by Congress, and the management of the reserves of the United States.
Section 2. The National Bank of the United States shall be independent in its management of the monetary policy of the United States.
Section 3. The Governor of the Bank of the United States shall be a senior officer of the council of state.
Section 4. Congress shall have the power to enforce this article by appropriate legislation. Legislation regarding the operations and regulation of the Bank of the United States shall be considered Organic Law.
Section 5. This article shall take effect five years after ratification, allowing time for the Federal Reserve, Federal Deposit Insurance Corporation, Comptroller General of the Currency, and all other relevant agencies and offices to be reestablished along the provisions of this amendment.
